Twelve Pillars

12 Pillars is a book that has inspired many, this was a book written by Jim Rohn and Chris Widener
After reflecting, here's how i think the reading could be applied and change my life.



Chapter 4 "Writing definite goals"

Chapter 4 speaks of writing definite goals, whether long term or short term. There’s a quote in the novel, it’s not about getting rich but who you become along the journey to get rich. after reading chapter 4,
instead of setting long-term goals, I will now set short-term objectives to reach towards those goals, with dates by which I should’ve accomplished those objectives, I will also celebrate the minor milestones because it is those milestones
that will motivate me to move on to the next objective. Another thing I will also do is write down those goals somewhere where I will see them and not stumble on them like in previous occurrences.



Chapter 5 "The proper use of time”

This chapter made me realize that the more you waste your days doing everything that is not aligned with your goals, is the closer you’ll get to stuck in the “system. I will ensure that as a college student I don’t get so caught up
trying to fit in or be a part of a cliché that I waste my time. The journey to becoming successful comes with one hard truth, the friends, or people you surround yourself with can determine who you become.



Chapter 6 “Surround yourself with the best people”

Personally, I can say this process will get lonely, because if I start to question the people I surround myself with, I will have to come to the hard truth that they don’t align with my goal, what I’ll do is slowly distant myself from these people,
I’ll try to show them the path I’ve taken and if they see this path as one to take then I’ll encourage them to do the same, but if not, then I’ll kindly ask them to respect my wishes to move on, I’ll be ready to accept the loneliness of this journey, if they wish to join, great,
if not then I’ll move on with my life and hope for nothing but the best for them.



Chapter 7 “Be a Life-Long Learner”

Many successful people today have read thousands of books and to date they still try to read or have meaningful conversations to widen the scope of their knowledge.
I will try to read more, that’s one of my many failures on my journey, and that’s to read. I will be sure to improve this.
